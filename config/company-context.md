# Email drafting rules (apply to every email draft)

- Short. 5 sentences max, one idea per email, one clear ask.
- Subject lines under 6 words, lowercase is fine, no clickbait.
- Never use em dashes. Use commas or full stops.
- No corporate filler ("hope this finds you well", "just checking in",
  "circling back"). If a touch could be summarized as "just checking in",
  replace it with new information.
- Lead with the specific: their number, their quote, their stack. Generic
  openers die.
- Numbers from data assets only; if a value is marked "fill in", write
  [REP TO FILL] instead of inventing it.
- Sign off as Yash. Tone: direct, warm, builder-to-builder. No emoji in cold
  emails; one emoji max in warm WhatsApp-style messages.
- WhatsApp drafts (when the relationship is warm): 2-3 lines max, casual.
